The Love Hypothesis summary link.

The Love Hypothesis by Ali Hazelwood (2021) follows a female scientist’s comedic journey to true love that’s fraught with lies, tears, and awkward moments. The book was an instant NY Times bestseller, a BuzzFeed Best Summer Read of 2021, and Goodreads Choice Awards finalist. Born in Italy, Ali Hazelwood moved to the United States via Japan and Germany to pursue a Ph.D. in neuroscience. She currently works as a college professor and writes romance novels about women in STEM fields. The Love Hypothesis was her debut novel. This guide follows the 2021 Berkley edition.

The Silent Patient is a psychological thriller that explores the mystery surrounding a woman named Alicia Berenson, who becomes mute after being accused of killing her husband. A psychotherapist, Theo Faber, becomes obsessed with uncovering the truth behind her silence.

Love On The Brain summary link

In this captivating rom-com from bestselling author Ali Hazelwood, Bee Königswasser—a determined scientist with a fierce admiration for Marie Curie—lands the opportunity of a lifetime: leading a neuroengineering project at NASA. However, her excitement quickly turns to dread upon realizing she must co-lead with her former rival, the irresistibly handsome Levi Ward. Their tumultuous past looms large as they navigate the intricacies of collaboration, but when mysterious equipment mishaps occur, Levi begins to shed his icy demeanor, offering unexpected support and igniting Bee's hopes. As she grapples with her feelings and the tantalizing chemistry between them, Bee must confront the ultimate question: what risks is she willing to take for love?

Me Before You summary

Me Before You by Jojo Moyes chronicles the burgeoning romance between Will Traynor, a quadriplegic man, and Louisa Clark, his caregiver. Published in 2012, Me Before You is the first novel in Moyes’s trilogy and is followed by After You (2015) and Still Me (2018). The novel was adapted into a 2016 film starring Emilia Clarke and Sam Claflin. Written from various perspectives, Me Before You explores the complexities of disability, love, and euthanasia. Content Warning:Me Before You references sexual assault, suicidal ideation, and assisted suicide. This guide also discusses the text’s controversial portrayal of people with disabilities.

The Traitors Game summary

In the treacherous kingdom of Antora, nothing is as it seems. Exiled for three years in the harsh Lava Fields, Kestra Dallisor finds herself reluctantly pulled back into the deadly games of palace intrigue, as her father serves the despotic King Lord Endrick. Captured by a group of rebels intent on using her to locate the fabled Olden Blade—the sole weapon capable of defeating the immortal king—Kestra refuses to play the docile pawn they expected. With her captor, Simon, struggling to keep her in line, the lines between ally and enemy blur, forcing both to question their loyalties as dark secrets unravel and the stakes rise. They must navigate a dangerous game of power, deception, and unexpected alliances in a fight for their lives and the fate of Antora.

The Deceiver's Heart summary

Kestra Dallisor has finally gained possession of the Olden Blade. With the dagger in her control, she attempts to destroy the tyrannical Lord Endrick. But when Kestra fails, the king strips her of her memory, and leaves her weak and uncertain, bound to obey him. Heartbroken, Simon is desperate to return Kestra to the rebel she was, but refuses to use magic to heal her. With untrusting Coracks and Halderians threatening to capture and kill her, and war looming on the horizon, Kestra and Simon will have to learn to trust each other again if they have any hope of surviving. But can a heart once broken ever be healed?

The Warrior's Curse summary

Simon and Kestra are hurtling down paths ever farther from each other as the battle for control of Antora rages. Simon, newly king of the Halderians, is pressed on all sides by royal duties. Harlyn Mindell is his betrothed and intended queen, but Simon can’t give up hope for a life with Kestra. Kestra, exiled, feels her magic growing — and despite everyone’s warnings, she knows she must embrace it. That power is the key to the kingdom’s future. Both still strive to defeat the evil Lord Endrick, but danger awaits them on the murky road forward. And is a common enemy enough to help them survive — or to carry them back to each other?

The Perks Of Being A Wallflower summary

The Perks of Being a Wallflower is a story told through a series of letters; the first one is dated August 25, 1991, and the last is dated August 23, 1992. Each letter is addressed the same—“Dear Friend.” Charlie is hoping this Friend, someone older but whom he has never met, will be a person he can trust although he only knows about him from a conversation he overheard. This Friend had the chance to take advantage of someone at a party but does not; this makes him a good person in Charlie’s eyes. It is a one-sided, sporadic correspondence covering Charlie’s first year of high school. In these letters, he shares his failures, successes, disappointments, questions, observations, and conclusions.
